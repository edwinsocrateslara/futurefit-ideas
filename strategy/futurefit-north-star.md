# FutureFit AI · Strategy — North Star

## How We Win

Three levers we pull to win.

We don't beat bigger competitors on individual features. We beat them because of how distribution, the engine, and proximity to dollars compound together.

**01 — Distribution: Scale and reach of our work and our brand.**
Massachusetts is our customer because they heard about Connecticut. The work is to keep turning every customer into the reference that opens the next one. That means deep work for deeply complicated systems.

**02 — The Engine: Transition graph + orchestration.**
Who has moved from A to B — by sector, geography, demographics, behavior — paired with the orchestration that moves a person through it. Nobody else is building that combination for our buyers.

**03 — Proximity to Dollars: Closer to how money flows.**
Today: helping buyers figure out how to pay us. In three years: pilots where their funding flows through us — paid to spend their dollars wisely, not to solve a technical problem.

## Must-Have Capabilities

Four reasons why customers choose us.

**Transition graph**
The data asset underneath everything: who moves from A to B, by sector, geography, demographics, and behavior. Necessary, but not sufficient on its own.

**Orchestration toward outcomes**
Profile a person, route them, intervene, follow through. This is where reps compound and where we get paid. Guidance only matters if it leads to measurably better outcomes.

**Enterprise-grade infrastructure**
The privacy, security, integrations, and technology as a service approach any government or NPO customer requires to buy. We already have it. The work is to keep it sharp.

**Multi-stakeholder town square**
Surface area for administrators, employers, providers, and individuals — tuned to each customer's local theory of change and funding connection points. Administrators first. Employers and providers second. Staff a distant third.

Payments proximity is on the watchlist. Could become a real capability later if we lean into the commercial moat.

## How We Get to 2031

### Where the IP lives

**Today:** IP is strongly oriented around recommenders and skills taxonomy rather than an engine.

**April 2028 (the foundation):** Engine is a real, named asset with one to two FTE of dedicated capacity; first version of a transition graph is lighting up in two or three subsets (e.g., one MA sector, one Ontario sector). Recommenders still do most of the surfacing in production.

**April 2031 (the bet realized):** Engine is the company's primary defensible IP, with a fully populated transition graph across our active jurisdictions, ingesting SLDS, UI wage data, third-party transition data, and intervention outcomes. Recommenders are a thin commodity layer the engine sits on top of.

### What the talent experience is

**Today:** User only shows up in white-labeled platform UI and must navigate their steps independently.

**April 2028 (the foundation):** Predominantly inside our white-labeled platform UI; first plug-in / menu-architecture experiments live in one jurisdiction. Roadmap steps replaced by a smarter, conversational progress flow on the talent portal.

**April 2031 (the bet realized):** User shows up wherever the jurisdiction has chosen — our white label, an integrated case management system, a partner provider, a general-purpose AI assistant via plug-in. We are indifferent to the surface and obsessed with progress.

### Where the dollars come from

**Today:** Long-term platform contracts.

**April 2028 (the foundation):** Long-term platform contracts (still ~80% of revenue), with first outcome-aligned services-as-SaaS pilots in two accounts. Annual contract values trending up; two states past $1M ACV.

**April 2031 (the bet realized):** Mix of platform contracts, admin fees on routed budgets, per-outcome pricing, and at least one philanthropic / state co-funded routing pilot at meaningful scale. Path to $30M with ~10 state-class anchors plus adjacent workforce dollars (SNAP E&T, outplacement, regional intermediaries).

### Where the data flywheel is

**Today:** Does not exist.

**April 2028 (the foundation):** Outcomes data captured for talent user; intervention data captured where the case management integration allows; training provider data still patchy. We can defend learning rate to a sophisticated buyer.

**April 2031 (the bet realized):** Outcomes, intervention, and provider data flowing in by default because data return is a condition of being on our marketplace. WIOA-grade outcome reporting is a feature, not just a one-off deliverable.

### How we are described

**Today:** "Career navigation for job seekers, powered by connective technology across siloed players in the workforce development ecosystem."

**April 2028 (the foundation):** "The technology Connecticut, Massachusetts, and Ontario use to connect modern employment services."

**April 2031 (the bet realized):** "The system North American workforce dollars flow through. The engine that turns workforce spend into measurable lifetime wage gain."

## Build Focus for the Next Year

**01 — Make data capture and workflows seamless to grow LX & ON SSM market share.**
Event capture, PIRL-compliant reporting, and clean data flows between FutureFit and state and case management systems. The employer portal holds the same bar — onboarding, registration, and talent recommendations have to work without complaint. Case management integration is the highest-risk and as-yet undefined item.
*Commercial stake:* The unlock for a state/province to consider buying, and what keeps them from churning. It's one play in how Ontario becomes more than one catchment.

**02 — Build tools for regional and industry intermediary town squares.**
Configuration that holds each customer's local theory of change — Ontario's three-stream logic is not Massachusetts's setup. Employer demand signals visible to admins and providers. Industry-specific skills and funding connection points filled.
*Commercial stake:* Renewal and expansion driver. The second play in how Ontario becomes more than one catchment.

**03 — Transform the talent experience from "matching" to "moving".**
Eliminate friction in job seeker registration. Proactively advance talent to the obvious next step, and automate full jobs to be done in response to a "how can I help?" front door for talent.
*Commercial stake:* Where reps compound. Better UX means fewer lost deals and better outcomes, and outcomes are what we eventually get paid on.

**04 — Build the progress and outcomes layer.**
Enable the data and links that lets the graph learn what drives outcomes. First progress and outcome views are in administrators' hands in a form they can act on. At least one validated case study — first- or third-party data — confirms a job outcome we sourced.
*Commercial stake:* Differentiation that no competitor can replicate quickly. It's also the precondition for any payments-proximity play down the road.
